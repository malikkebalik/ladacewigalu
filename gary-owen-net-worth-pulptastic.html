What is Gary Owen’s Net Worth?

Gary Owen, the American actor and stand-up comedian, boasts a net worth of $4 million. He gained fame in 1997 on the stand-up comedy TV show “ComicView.” Since then, he has starred in numerous comedy films, including “Held Up,” “Daddy Day Care,” “Little Man,” “College,” and “Think Like a Man.”

Owen has a significant African-American fanbase thanks to his appearances on BET. In fact, Ebony magazine even gave him the title “Black America’s Favorite White Comic.”

Gary Owen’s Early Life

Gary Owen was born on July 26, 1974 in Cincinnati, Ohio. He grew up with his six siblings in a trailer park in Oxford.

Service in the US Navy

As a young adult, Owen served for six years in the US Navy as a Master-at-Arms. He was in the Presidential Honor Guard as well.

Owen’s Stand-Up Career Takes Off After Military Service

Following his military service, Gary Owen began pursuing stand-up comedy in San Diego. His performances quickly gained a significant African-American following, earning him recognition and accolades. This success eventually led him to a gig at the renowned Comedy Store in West Hollywood.

Owen Makes History as First White Host of BET’s “ComicView”

In 1997, Owen’s career took off after his appearances on the BET stand-up comedy show “ComicView.” He was given his own one-hour showcase, and later became the host of the program after being chosen from four finalists. This made him the first and only white man to host “ComicView.”

Gary Owen’s Comedy Specials

Gary Owen has released several comedy specials on DVD, including “Breakin’ Out the Park” and “Urban Legend.” In addition, he has also released “Gary Owen: True Story,” “Gary Owen: I Agree with Myself,” and “Gary Owen: #DoinWhatIDo” as stand-up specials.

Owen’s popularity with African-American audiences has led to him being named “Black America’s Favorite White Comic” by Ebony magazine in 2011.

Comedic Film Career

Owen made his film debut in the 1999 buddy crime comedy “Held Up,” and went on to have bit parts in various films including “Daddy Day Care” and “Love Chronicles.” He had a bigger role in 2008’s “College” and appeared in the ensemble cast of Tim Story’s “Think Like a Man” in 2012. Owen reunited with Story and Kevin Hart for “Ride Along” in 2014 and reprised his role in “Think Like a Man Too” the same year. He has appeared in several other films, including “Welcome to Sudden Death” in 2020 and “The House Next Door: Meet the Blacks 2” in 2021.

Television Appearances

Gary Owen has appeared in various television shows throughout his career. In 1998, he had a role in the sitcom “The Wayans Bros.” He also played Zach in the fifth season of the Tyler Perry sitcom “House of Payne” in 2009. In 2016, Owen was given his own BET show called “The Gary Owen Show.” Additionally, he made an appearance in an episode of “Nubbin & Friends” in the same year.

Family Life

Gary Owen married Kenya Duke in 2003, and they had two children together – a son named Austin and a daughter named Kennedy. The couple divorced in 2021. Duke also has a son named Emilio from a previous relationship.
