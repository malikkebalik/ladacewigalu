Linda Blair Wiki Biography

Born on January 22, 1959, in St. Louis, Missouri USA, Linda Denise Blair gained fame for her portrayal of the haunted girl Regan in the film “The Exorcist” (1973), for which she received an Oscar nomination and won a Golden Globe award. In addition to her acting career, Blair has authored the book “Going Vegan” (2001).

Blair started her career as a child model and commercial actress at the age of six before gaining widespread recognition for her role in “The Exorcist.” She also starred in the sequel, “The Exorcist: The Heretic” (1977), which contributed significantly to her net worth.

Other noteworthy roles include appearances in TV movies such as “Born Innocent” (1974) and “Sarah T. – Portrait of a Teenage Alcoholic” (1975).

After encountering personal and professional challenges, Blair founded the non-profit organization Linda Blair World Heart Foundation, which focuses on animal rescue and welfare, reflecting her advocacy for compassionate causes.
