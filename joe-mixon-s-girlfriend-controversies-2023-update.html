A Little About Joe Mixon

Joe (fully named Joseph Tyler Mixon) was born on July 24, 1996, under the star sign of Leo in Antioch, California. He is a bulky man with a physically toned body structure. 

Mixon stands tall at the towering height of 6 feet 1 inch (1.85 meters) and weighs 220 lbs. (100 kg). 

According to the sources, Joe’s parents are Alise Mixon and John Mixon. However, he was raised by his single mother alongside his five siblings. 

Education And Career

For his early academics, Joe Mixon attended Freedom High School in Oakley, California. Joe played football and basketball with his high school team, where he was a three-year starter at running back. 

By his high school graduation, Joe stood as a five-star recruit and the top-ranked running back in the nation. Altogether, he had 47 offers, and keeping everything aside, he committed to the University of Oklahoma. 

Joe began his collegiate in 2014 and only played with them for two years, till 2016. Moving on, he decided to forgo his remaining two years of college and entered the 2017 NFL Draft. 

The Cincinnati Bengals selected him as the 48th overall pick in the second round. The same year, Joe signed his contract with the Bengals for four years, worth $5.45 million with $2.81 million guaranteed and a signing bonus of $2.10 million. 

In September 2017, Joe Mixon made his NFL debut and has been playing for the Bengals since then. 

Controversy

Whoever knew Joe Mixon had heard of his controversies since high school and collegiate days.

In 2014, he was charged with misdemeanor assault after he hit Amelia Molitor at a restaurant in Norman, Oklahoma.

The authorities sentenced him to 100 hours of community service with a year of probation with counseling. 

In 2016, Joe Mixon faced legal trouble after an altercation with a parking attendant. He then tried to intimidate the officer with his car, ripped his citation in half, and threw its pieces in the attendant’s face.

Arrest Warrent

On February 2, 2023, an arrest warrant was issued for Mixon after he allegedly pointed a gun at a woman in downtown Cincinnati last month.
