Wiz Khalifa: A Deeper Look

Known to the public as Wiz Khalifa, Cameron Jibril Thomaz is a well-known American rap artist, singer, songwriter, and actor. He entered the music scene in 2006 with his album “Show and Prove” under the “Rostrum Records” label. After its release, the album received positive reviews and sold over 10,000 copies. Wiz Khalifa rose to prominence in 2010 with his hit single “Black and Yellow,” featured in his third studio album “Rolling Papers.” The track reached the #1 spot on the Billboard Hot 100 and sold over three million digital copies. His fifth studio album, “Blacc Hollywood,” released in 2014, garnered approximately 90,000 in sales during its debut week and featured collaborations with notable artists like Snoop Dogg, Nas, Nicki Minaj, and Ghost Loft.

Wiz Khalifa’s Financial Status: $30 Million

How wealthy is Wiz Khalifa? In 2012, his annual income was reported to be $9 million, and by 2013 he was earning as much as $100,000 per show. Notable assets in his possession include a $900,000 house in Canonsburg and a $31,000 Dodge Challenger SRT 8. The majority of Wiz Khalifa’s $30 million net worth has been accumulated through his successful rap career.

Born in North Dakota in 1987, Wiz Khalifa’s parents’ military careers led to frequent relocations during his childhood, eventually settling in Pittsburgh. Inspired by The Notorious B.I.G and Jimi Hendrix, he pursued a career in rap, joining “Rostrum Records” in 2004. His debut mixtape “Prince of the City: Welcome to Pistolvania” contributed significantly to the release of his first album “Show and Prove.” Following this success, his album “Deal or No Deal” sold over 167,000 CDs in the United States. To date, Wiz Khalifa has released five studio albums and three collaborative albums.

In 2012, Khalifa ventured into acting with appearances in “Gangs of Roses 2: Next Generation” and the stoner film “Mac & Devin Go to High School,” alongside Snoop Dogg and Mike Epps. Currently, he is filming for the movie “High School 2,” scheduled for release in 2015.
